The Supply Chain Crisis Unfolded

The pandemic reinvented the supply chain in 2021. According to Lazaro Gamio and Peter S. Goodman (New York Times, Dec. 5th, 2021), Covid 19 has reshaped today’s world of the supply chain. Their article mentions ships stuck at sea, warehouses overflowing, trucks without drivers created a crisis earlier than expected.

ECommerce has grown into what specialties believed would happen over 5-10 years in the last year. All industries suffered rapid changes with little to no action time. The supply chain faced a significant crisis in which either company adapted or broke. “The turmoil has revealed how the need to ship surgical masks to West Africa from China can have a cascading effect on Ford’s ability to put back-up cameras on its cars at factories in Ohio and delay the arrival of Amazon Prime orders in Florida in time for the holidays,” stated Gamio and Goodman. All events, however, can be traced down to the beginning of the pandemic.
